The missing-assignment spiral

A small gap doesn't stay small.

We're not here to scare you — this is one of the most common things we see. But it helps to understand how a little slip becomes a big one.

1 → 5

One missing assignment becomes five, fast. Then grades drop, motivation drops, and avoidance takes over — because catching up now feels impossible.

Still developing

The part of the brain that runs planning, organizing, and follow-through keeps developing all through the teen years. This is a skill gap — not laziness.

Questions parents ask first

Is this just tutoring?
No. Tutoring re-teaches content for an hour and then disappears until next week. On Track manages the process — organization, accountability, and follow-through — so the work actually gets done. It can work right alongside a tutor.
What ages is this for?
Middle and high school students — roughly grades 6 through 12 — who are capable but inconsistent, disorganized, or overwhelmed.
How often will you work with my child?
Most students start with 2 sessions a week (our Core program) or 3 (Intensive), depending on how much support they need. We'll recommend the right level on your consultation.
Do you actually work with their teachers and portals?
Yes. We help track assignments and deadlines and keep the whole academic process organized, so nothing falls through the cracks.
Will my child even listen to you?
Usually, yes. When accountability comes from an outside adult whose only job is the academic process, the dynamic changes — and it finally gets you out of the role of constant enforcer.
Isn't my child just being lazy?
Almost never. It's usually executive-function skills that are still developing, plus genuine overwhelm. The fix is structure and accountability — not more pressure.
What happens on the consultation?
It's a relaxed parent call. We learn what's going on, tell you honestly whether we can help, and explain how the program works. No pressure to sign up.
